Research

My research sits at the intersection of control theory, motion planning, and AI — building robots that are provably safe and genuinely useful in human-centered environments. I am particularly interested in applying Control Barrier Functions to healthcare robotics, multimodal teleoperation, pipelines for mobile manipulation. Papers I am a primary contributor on are highlighted.

Safety-Critical Robot Control with CBF-MPC-Transformer: A Unified Framework for Human-Safe Autonomous Systems

8th International Conference on Industry of the Future and Smart Manufacturing, 2024
arXiv (coming soon)

A unified framework combining Control Barrier Functions, MPC, and a Transformer-based human-intent predictor to enforce hard safety constraints during human-robot collaboration, validated on hospital AMR scenarios.
