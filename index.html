Samuel Oluwakorede Oyefusi

I am a graduate student in the Robotics Engineering program at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), where I study human-robot interaction, safety-critical control, and autonomous manipulation. I am affiliated with Pan-Atlantic University, Lagos, Nigeria, and hold a P.E. (Professional Engineer) and PMP® certification.

My research sits at the crossroads of control theory, motion planning, and AI — building robots that are provably safe and genuinely useful in demanding, human-centered settings. I am particularly interested in Control Barrier Function (CBF) frameworks for healthcare robotics, teleoperation interfaces, and ROS 2 simulation pipelines. I am pursuing PhD opportunities aligned with the work of Prof. Berk Calli and Prof. Cagdas Onal at WPI.

Beyond research, I am a community leader and SDG advocate. I co-founded Ridebuddy, serve as a Global Ambassador of Theirworld, and was president of the Living Green Club at Pan-Atlantic University, where I led smart irrigation and community resilience projects. My work is motivated by a belief that robotics and AI can meaningfully advance quality education, clean energy, and health outcomes across Africa and beyond.

Research & Publications

My research spans human-robot interaction, safety-critical control (CBF, MPC), motion planning (RRT, A*, Hybrid A*), and ROS 2 simulation for mobile manipulation. I aim to make autonomous systems verifiably safe and deployable in real-world healthcare and industrial settings. Papers with significant contributions are highlighted.

CBF · MPC
Transformer
Safety-Critical Robot Control with CBF-MPC-Transformer: A Unified Framework for Human-Safe Autonomous Systems
Samuel Oluwakorede Oyefusi, Peter Onu, Anup Pradhan
8th International Conference on Industry of the Future and Smart Manufacturing, 2024   ★ Highlighted
arXiv (coming soon)

We propose a unified framework combining Control Barrier Functions (CBFs), Model Predictive Control (MPC), and a Transformer-based prediction module to guarantee safety during human-robot collaboration. The framework enforces hard safety constraints in real time while adapting to human intent predictions, demonstrated on hospital AMR scenarios.

Samuel Oluwakorede Oyefusi

A systematic survey of how the robotics community has migrated from ROS 1 to ROS 2, covering middleware comparisons, tooling ecosystems, simulation integrations (Gazebo Harmonic, Ignition), and adoption patterns across mobile manipulation, aerial robotics, and HRI subfields.

VCPF
HRI
A Value-Centered Progression Framework for Consumer Human-Robot Interaction
Samuel Oluwakorede Oyefusi, Peter Onu
Under review

Introduces VCPF, a staged framework mapping user values (safety, trust, utility) to robot capability levels. Validated through user studies on telepresence and domestic service robots, providing actionable design guidelines for consumer HRI products at different autonomy levels.

Trustworthy
Robotics
Towards Trustworthy Human-Robot Interaction: A Systematic Review of Safety, Transparency, and Accountability
Samuel Oluwakorede Oyefusi, Peter Onu
Under review

Synthesizes 80+ papers on human-robot trust across industrial, healthcare, and domestic settings. Proposes a structured taxonomy of trust-building mechanisms and identifies gaps where formal verification tools (CBFs, runtime monitors) can close the bridge between trust and safety.
